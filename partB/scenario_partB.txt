Part B: Scenario Where LLM and SMT Solver Disagree
====================================================

Scenario Name: "Alex — The 8-Month Roommate"

Taxpayer: Dave (age 38, U.S. citizen, not a dependent of anyone else)
Potential Dependent: Alex (age 35)

Facts:
  - Relationship: Alex is Dave's longtime friend; no family relationship whatsoever.
  - Residence: Alex moved in with Dave in May and lived there through December
                — exactly 8 months out of the 12-month tax year.
  - Gross income: Alex earned $3,000 this year (well below the $4,700 exemption amount).
  - Support: Dave paid for Alex's rent, food, and bills.
              Dave provided approximately 65% of Alex's total support.
              Alex provided approximately 35% of his own support.
  - Joint return: Alex did not file a joint return (he is single).
  - Citizenship: Alex is a U.S. citizen.
  - Note: Alex is not a qualifying child of any taxpayer (he is 35 years old).

Question: Is Alex a dependent of Dave for federal tax purposes?

------------------------------------------------------------
LLM Answer (without Chain-of-Thought)
------------------------------------------------------------

Prompt to LLM:
> Dave is 38 years old. Alex is Dave's 35-year-old friend who lived with Dave for 8 months
> of the tax year. Dave provided 65% of Alex's total support. Alex earned $3,000 this year.
> Alex did not file a joint return and is a U.S. citizen. Dave is not anyone's dependent.
> Is Alex a dependent of Dave under 26 USC §152? Answer yes or no.

LLM Answer (no CoT): YES — Alex is a dependent.

LLM Reasoning (no CoT):
The LLM responded that Alex qualifies because: Dave provides more than half of Alex's support
(65% > 50%), Alex's income is below the exemption amount ($3,000 < $4,700), and Alex is a
U.S. citizen who did not file a joint return. The LLM identified these as the key "qualifying
relative" requirements and concluded Alex is a dependent.

------------------------------------------------------------
SMT Solver (Z3) Answer
------------------------------------------------------------

Z3 Result: Alex is NOT a dependent.

Key Z3 findings:
  - is_qualifying_child: False
      → Age test fails (35 is not < 19, not < 24+student, not disabled)
      → Relationship test fails (friend is not child/sibling)
  - is_qualifying_relative: False
      → qr_relationship_test: FALSE  ← critical failure
          * is_listed_relative: False (friend is not an enumerated relative)
          * full_year_household: False (only 8 months, NOT the entire year)
      → qr_gross_income: True ($3,000 < $4,700) ✓
      → qr_claimant_support: True (0.65 > 0.5) ✓
      → qr_not_qc: True (not a QC of anyone) ✓
  - is_dependent: False

The relationship test is the decisive failure point.

------------------------------------------------------------
These answers differ because...
------------------------------------------------------------

The LLM overlooked a critical requirement of §152(d)(2):

  A qualifying relative must be either:
    (A) one of the enumerated relatives (child, parent, sibling, grandparent, etc.), OR
    (B) a member of the taxpayer's household for the ENTIRE taxable year — all 12 months.

Alex is NOT an enumerated relative (he is a friend, not a family member).
Alex lived with Dave for only 8 months — not the full 12 months required for option (B).

The LLM focused on the three "easy" qualifying relative conditions that Alex passes
(income < exemption, support > 50%, no joint return) and missed the two-part
relationship requirement. Because Alex is a non-relative who did not live with Dave
for the entire year, he fails the qualifying relative test entirely.

The SMT encoding captures this precisely:
  (define-fun json_qr_relationship () Bool
    (or is_listed_relative full_year_household))

where `full_year_household` is false (8 months ≠ 12 months) and
`is_listed_relative` is false (friend ≠ enumerated family member).
